Rasmalai

Description:

Rasmalai is a soft, spongy Indian dessert made of paneer discs soaked in saffron- and cardamom-flavored sweetened milk. Garnished with nuts, it’s served chilled and melts in the mouth—a delicate and luxurious sweet often enjoyed during festivals.

Ingredients:

Full-fat milk, lemon juice or vinegar, sugar, water, saffron strands, cardamom powder, almonds, pistachios.

Steps:

  1. Boil 1 liter of milk. Once it comes to a boil, add lemon juice or vinegar to curdle it. Strain using a muslin cloth and wash with water. Hang for 30–45 minutes to remove excess water.
  2. Knead the chenna until smooth. Make small, flat discs (rasgullas). Ensure there are no cracks for a soft texture.
  3. Boil water with sugar to make a light syrup. Add the rasgulla discs and cook for 15–20 minutes until they double in size. Let them cool.
  4. Boil 1 liter of milk and simmer until it reduces to half. Add sugar, cardamom powder, saffron strands, and chopped nuts like pistachios and almonds.
  5. Gently squeeze the cooked rasgullas to remove syrup, then place them in the rabri. Let them soak and absorb the flavor for a few hours.
  6. Refrigerate rasmalai for a few hours. Garnish with more nuts and saffron. Serve chilled for a rich, creamy Bengali dessert experience.
